TLC Confirms Broadway Play With 'Hamilton' Team And TV Show Are Coming

TLC has been quite busy, and are now teasing their upcoming endeavors with their devoted fanbase.

At ’90s Con, held over the weekend (Sept. 15-17), T-Boz and Chilli appeared on a panel where T-Boz shared, “We’re working on a Broadway play.”

According to PEOPLE, Chilli added, “The cool thing, we do have a lot of people that worked on ‘Hamilton‘ working with us for ours.” When speaking on other new avenues, T-Boz chimed back in, “That means we’ll have music for the play, and a TV show we can’t talk about.”

The duo first hinted about the play in 2019. “We didn’t tell everything. We have so many stories to tell,” T-Boz explained to Chatter. “We didn’t tell it all in our biopic we did with VH1. A lot of good stuff. It’s gonna be very emotional but a good ride.”

The update regarding their theatrical debut follows the conclusion of their Hot Summer Nights tour and comes three months after the release of their Lifetime documentary, TLC Forever.

The two-hour film goes deeper than their 2013 biopic, CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story, as T-Boz and Chilli not only divulge about their joint career, but also their current personal lives, including discussing T-Boz’s brain tumor and Chilli revealing she was the mastermind behind their infamous November 1994 VIBE cover.

When speaking on their legacy, T-Boz said in the documentary, “Three little Black girls from the hood [became] the biggest and the best-selling girl group of all time in America and still hold that title, we did that and we still hold the torch.”
