Logan Paul Net Worth: A YouTube Actor Turned Boxer

Logan Alexander Paul is a famous American Podcaster, YouTuber, and Actor turned professional boxer. The internet celebrity has a global following for his YouTube videos; he has more than 22 million subscribers on the platform.

The 26-year-old only fought two times, out of which only one was a professional bout. He is the older brother of internet celebrity-turned boxer, Jake Paul. In the ring, Logan Paul uses the nickname ‘Maverick‘.

He’s also just founded a fashion line, showing the world that he will be something big when he reaches his late 20’s.

Before Rising to Stardom

Before his fame, Logan Paul was a high-school athlete. He was an all-star football player and had many wins at wrestling. He got fifth place in OHSAA State Championships during his senior high and got into Ohio University as an outcome.

Logan Paul went one better in the university by qualifying for the school wrestling championship.

He ultimately dropped out to follow a career on YouTube, which has brought him a lot of success. His vlogs, which sometimes featured his parents, garnered an international audience. Logan Paul has performed in many movies like Airplane Mode and Foursome. He now concentrates his attention on his Podcast, ‘The Impaulsive’, which has over 3.1 Million followers on YouTube.

The 26-year-old sensation has totally shifted his attention to the sport of boxing.

Logan Paul and KSI Boxing Matches

Logan Paul and another YouTuber, KSI, famously dislike each other. On August 25, 2018, KSI and Logan held a pay-per-view boxing match promoted as “the biggest internet event in history” A rematch took place on November 9, 2019, at the Staples Center in Los Angeles.

The first fight was viewed by 2.25 million spectators live, with 1 million watching through pay-per-view, and another estimation shows that 1.2 million watching illegally via Twitch streams.

In the end, 1.3 million purchased the fight via PPV, generating around $13 million in revenue. Logan and KSI likely earned around $2 million from the match before taxes when all other sources like merchandise are included.

For the second fight, they each were guaranteed a minimum of $900,000. Their total earnings for the second fight, which boxing company DAZN USA broadcasted, likely topped $3 million each.
