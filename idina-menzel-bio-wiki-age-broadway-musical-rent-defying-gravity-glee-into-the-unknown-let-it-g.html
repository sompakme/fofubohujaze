Idina Menzel Bio-Wiki

Idina Kim Menzel (born May 30th, 1971) is an American actress, singer, and songwriter. Idina came to the limelight when she played the character of Maurene Johnson in the Broadway musical Rent. Her performance earned her a Tony Award nomination in 1996. She reprised the role in the musical’s 2005 film adaptation. In 2003, she originated the character of Elphaba in the Broadway musical Wicked, a performance for which she won the 2004 Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical. She came back to Broadway as Elizabeth Vaughan in 2014 musical which earned her a third Tony Award nomination.

Menzel is also famously known for playing Shelby Corcoran on the musical dramedy TV series Glee from 2010 – 2013 and Nancy Tremaine in the 2007 Disney live-action/animated film Enchanted. She also voiced Queen Elsa in Disney’s hit 2013 3D CGI-animated musical film Frozen, in which she sang the Oscar-and Grammy Award-winning song Let It Go.

The song reached number 5 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, making her the first Tony Award-winning actress to reach the top 10. In 2019, Menzel reprised her voice character as Elsa in Frozen II. Her album Holiday Wishes was unleashed in 2014 and reached number 6 on the Billboard 200.

Idina Menzel Education

Kim went to J. Irving Baylis Elementary School in Plainview, New York, and then Syosset’s H.B. Thompson Middle School and Syosset High School. When Kim was 15 years old, her parents divorced, and she started working as a wedding and bar singer, a job that she continued throughout her time at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts, where she earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Drama in 1992.

Idina Menzel Career

Kim is a singer/songwriter and actress by profession. She began her professional career with performing in theatre musical, ”Rent” in 1994. Kim represented Maureen Johnson which earned her Tony Award nomination. She replayed the character in 1996 in Nederlander Theatre. Then in 1998, she unleashed her debut album, Still, I Can’t Be Still. Kim’s singing career was followed by other albums, Here and I Stand in 2004 and 2008 respectively.

Afterward, she unleashed Live: Barefoot at the Symphony in 2012, Holiday Wishes in 2014, and Kim in 2016. Back in her acting career, she did various other theatres plays like ”The Wild Party” in 2000, ”Hair,” and ”Aida” in 2001. She also played in plays like ”Wicked,” ”See What I Wanna See,” ”If/Then,” and others. Kim’s performance in Wicked won her a TGony award. Additionally, she also acted in TV shows and films. She featured in the film, ”Kissing Jessica Stein” in 2001 and ”The Tollbooth” in 2004.

She reprised the character of Maureen Johnson from the play in the film of the same name, ”Rent” in 2005. Some of her other movies are ”Enchanted,” ”Frozen,” and others. She voiced Elsa and sang, ”Let It Go” in the film ”Frozen” in 2013. Furthermore, she performed in various TV shows. Some of them are ”Glee”, ”Arthur,” ”Private Practice,” and so on.

Idina Menzel Defying Gravity

Defying Gravity is the signature song from the musical Wicked, composed by Stephen Schwartz, originally recorded by Idina and Kristin Chenoweth on May 10th, 2003, and unleashed on December 16th, 2003. It is mostly a solo sung by the main character of the show, Elphaba with two small duets at the beginning and in the middle of the song between Elphaba and her friend Glinda, and a chorus part at the end in which the citizens of Oz sing.
