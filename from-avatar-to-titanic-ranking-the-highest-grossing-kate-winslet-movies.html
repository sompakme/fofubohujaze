Highlights

  • In February, Kate Winslet spoke about her experience with fame after Titanic, expressing gratitude, but describing the scrutiny as "horrible."
  • Finding Neverland was the first Kate Winslet movie to cross the eight-figure mark, earning over $128 million globally.
  • Winslet's movies, including narrations and ensemble roles, have grossed a whopping $6.7 billion, solidifying her status as a Hollywood legend.

People often think Titanic was Kate Winslet's first movie – a reasonable inference, as that film instantly catapulted Winslet to worldwide fame.

On February 12th, Net-A-Porter published a wide-ranging interview with the star, in which Winslet addressed the impact of Titanic on her career.

With respect to the aftermath of Titanic, Winslet recalled in part:

"[After Titanic was released] I felt like I had to look a certain way, or be a certain thing, and because media intrusion was so significant at that time, my life was quite unpleasant ..."

"... guess what, being famous was horrible. I was grateful, of course. I was in my early twenties, and I was able to [afford] a flat. But I didn’t want to be followed [by reporters while I was] literally feeding the ducks."

However, her actual debut was in Peter Jackson's Heavenly Creatures, an engaging film based on real-life events, focused on the unusually close (and eventually violent) relationship between two teenage girls in the 1950s.

A heavenly beauty herself, Kate Winslet's work on Heavenly Creatures and caught the attention of the directors, due to her incredible acting skill and stunning presence on the screen.

After a brief role in A Kid In King Arthur's Court, her landmark performance in Sense And Sensibility affirmed her acting reputation, solidifying her status as a versatile and accomplished actress.

Kate did not stop there; she delivered back-to-back unforgettable performances, in Jude and Hamlet, before securing a massive career-boosting role in Titanic.

After Titanic, Winslet never slowed down, building her filmography diligently until she became a legend in Hollywood.

As a leading actress, Winslet's movies have grossed over $3.2 billion, and through her supporting roles, her filmmakers brought in $3.1 billion.

All Kate Winslet movies, including those where she served as a narrator and lead ensemble member, together have grossed a whopping $6.7 billion.

Let's take a peek at the highest-grossing movies of Kate Winslet's career, and examine how these films impacted her trajectory in Tinseltown.

Sense And Sensibility’s Total Gross: $134.5 Million

Sense And Sensibility is, at its core, a classic and apt depiction of life, emotions, and heartbreak, all of which were Austen's forte.

It was the third movie in Kate Winslet's career, and her portrayal of Marianne Dashwood earned her the first Oscar nomination, in the category of "Best Supporting Actress."

6 Contagion (2011)

Contagion’s Total Gross: $139.2 Million

Contagion was a stunningly prescient movie, one of the few films to re-enter and top the charts nearly a decade after its release:

The reason for its astonishing second act success was unfortunately tied to current events – released in 2011, Contagion eerily and accurately depicted a worldwide pandemic, nine years before a novel Coronavirus was discovered in Wuhan, China.

Contagion foretold a nearly identical pandemic, and imagined how people might react to it; similarities between real life and the film were astounding and unsettling.

It quickly became a worldwide sensation as the pandemic erupted, when neither people nor governments had any clue what to do; ultimately, it became something of a reference point in those early days.

In 2020, pandemic-related mitigation measures began in mid-March, but on March 5th, the New York Times reported that Contagion had begun "climbing the charts" on streaming platforms:

"According to Warner Bros., ['Contagion'] was listed as No. 270 among its catalog titles at the end of December [2019]. Since the start of 2020, it has jumped to second, bested only by Harry Potter movies. 'Contagion' is also trending on Amazon Prime Video and has flirted with the iTunes top 10."
