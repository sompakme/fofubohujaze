Bob Proctor was a Canadian self-help author, motivational speaker, entrepreneur, business counsellor and consultant, writer, and lecturer who was best known for his New York Times best-selling book You Were Born Rich.

Bob Proctor was born on July 5, 1934, in Toronto, Canada. He dropped out from Danforth Collegiate and Technical Institute after a bandsaw-inflicted thumb injury. He later joined the Nightingale-Conant Organization and worked his way up within the company.

Bob Proctor involved himself in writing and in 1984, his book You Were Born Rich, which he long strived to publish, was published by McCrary Publishing.

Bob Proctor is best known for co-founding the Proctor Gallagher Institute. He has written more than 40 books and worked in a film named Beyond the Secret.
