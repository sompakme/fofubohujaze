10 Longest Lasting Relationships, Ranked

TLC's 90 Day Fiance has captivated millions of viewers who get an inside look at couples who applied for a K-1 visa and have just 90 days to marry each other. Fans of the show have seen the good and bad of international romances, including how tough families can be welcoming someone from a foreign country, cultural differences and of course, the pressure to get married in just three months of entering the United States before one of them must go back to their country.

While viewers have seen the ups and downs of these types of long-distance relationships, there have actually been a number of successful marriages that have come out of the show. These couples have found a way to work through the difficulties and challenges of having an international long-distance relationship and have come out strong, even having a few kids! Here are 10 of the longest-lasting relationships from the show.

10 David And Annie

David and Annie appeared on the fifth season of 90 Day Fiance and at first, fans were skeptical of their relationship because David was struggling financially and they have a big age gap, with David being 48 at the time, and Annie, 24. However, too many surprised fans, they have remained as one of the strongest couples from the show, having gotten married in 2017, and now living in Scottsdale, Arizona.

Today, Annie and David look happily in love, even starring on the show's spinoff, 90 Day Fiance: Pillow Talk where fans are invited into the homes of former couples who watch the latest seasons of the show.

9 Josh And Aika

Josh and Aika starred on season five of the reality show and met on accident on a dating app. After meeting in person, Aika moved to America to be with Josh and the two ultimately tied the knot in 2017. Fans were unsure if the two would make it since Aika wanted kids and Josh revealed that he had gotten a vasectomy and even more, had a criminal record.

However, the two are still together and according to Soap Dirt, Aika revealed that they are "trying" for a baby, adding that "maybe next year or two years we will do it in the Philippines."

8 Loren And Alexei

In the third season of the show, fans met Lorena and Alexei who have been together since 2013 after Loren met him on a birthright trip to Israel. The two seemed to remain out of any major drama while on the show and later tied the knot in 2015, and had a second marriage in 2016, in Alexei's home country.

Since the show, fans saw the couple on 90 Day Fiance: Pillow Talk and 90 Day Fiance: Happily Ever After. Loren recently gave birth to their first child in April, a baby boy named Shai.

7 Brett And Daya

Fans met Brett and Daya on season two of the reality show and just like other couples, saw their fair share of ups and downs. Brett's mother was extremely unsupportive of her son's relationship with Daya, and it got more difficult as Daya tried her best to form a bond with Brett's six-year-old daughter.

While working through these issues, the couple still tied the knot in 2015, and are still together. In 2017, Brett and Daya welcomed a baby girl named Isabelle, and Daya now has a good relationship with Brett's daughter, Cassidy.

6 Danny And Amy

Fans watched Danny and Amy on season two of 90 Day Fiance and were well-liked by fans because unlike some couples, they really appeared to be in it for the long-run. Amy eventually moved from South Africa to the U.S. to be with Danny and the two got married in 2014, despite his father's close-minded beliefs.

The two shared a religious connection that has continued to grow after the show, moving to Texas, welcoming two children in 2015 and 2017, and launching a church in 2019.

5 Justin And Evelyn

Justin and Evelyn appeared on season two of 90 Day Fiance and it was a rocky beginning for the pair when she came to the U.S. and met Justin's family, who were not very supportive of the relationship.

However, they are one of the longest-lasting couples from the show, getting married in 2014, and will celebrate their sixth wedding anniversary this August. Recently, Evelyn shared that they will be expecting their first child together!

4 Mike and Aziza

On the show's first-ever season, fans met Mike, from Ohio, and Aziza, from Russia, who initially met in a language program. While it appeared to fans that the two wouldn't hit it off, Aziza eventually moved to the U.S. to be with Mike in the summer of 2014.

Today, the couple is married and has a daughter named Olivia Joan. In fact, every couple on the show's first season is still going strong!

3 Louis And Aya

Fans met Louis and Aya back in 2014 after she left her entire family in the Phillippines to be with him in the United States. While Louis was willing to move to the Phillippines for her, it was best for the both of them to live in the U.S., since Louis has two sons.

The couple is one of the longest-lasting married couples on the show, getting hitched in 2014, and welcoming a baby boy in 2018.

2 Russ And Paola

Fan favorites, Russ and Paola were on the first season of 90 Day Fiance and are still together since getting married in 2013. Paola is originally from Colombia, and met Russ while he was traveling for work.

The two have their fair share of ups and downs on the show and remain one of the most entertaining couples. Today, the reality stars live in Miami, Florida where they are raising their son, Axel, who was born on New Year's Day 2019.

1 Alan And Kirlyam

Alan, who is from Los Angeles, fell in love with Kirlyam, who is originally from Brazil. The stars were on the show's first season and have just celebrated their sixth wedding anniversary!

According to CheatSheet, Alan actually knew Kirlyam since she was 12, after visiting Brazil on a Mormon mission, but the two made it clear that they did not have a romantic relationship, and that they didn't get together until she was 18. Today, the couple is doing quite well and have a 2-year-old son together named Liam.
