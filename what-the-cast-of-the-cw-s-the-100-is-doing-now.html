What The Cast Of The CWs The 100 Is Doing Now

The CW network is known for adapting young adult stories into hit television shows. The Vampire Diaries and Nancy Drew are just two examples of successful young adult shows on the network. The 100 was incredibly successful for the CW, running for 7 seasons from 2014 to 2020. The story follows Clarke Griffin as she and 100 other teenagers are sent down from a spacecraft to see if Earth is once again habitable. The children are met with many challenges, especially when they learn that they are not alone on the planet.

The sci-fi show was a fan-favorite amongst CW viewers. The cast found themselves launched into popularity. Since the show’s ending in 2020, fans are eager to know what the actors and actresses plan to do next. Here’s what the cast has been up to since The 100 ended and what they are doing now.

8 Henry Ian Cusick In MacGyver

Henry Ian Cusick played Marcus Kane, one of the wiser characters and few adults to be in The 100. Fans loved his character’s relationship with Paige Turco’s Abigail Griffin and were devastated with his character’s death in season 6 of the show.

Since his exit from The 100, Cusick has been very busy with acting jobs. He played Russ Taylor in the television show MacGyver. He also has many film in post-production, including 1 Million Followers and Awaken. He will also do voice work in the animated show The Wingfeather Saga.

7 Lola Flanery As A Queen In Shadowhunters

Lola Flanery portrayed Madi, Clarke Griffin’s adoptive daughter. Madi entered The 100 in the season finale of season 4 and remains a main character of the show until its completion. She was initially portrayed by Imogen Tear in season 4, but during the show’s hiatus they re-cast the character to Flanery.

Though Flanery has not taken on any big roles since the end of The 100, fans still have plenty of options where they can see her phenomenal acting. Flanery had a brief run on Freeform’s Shadowhunters, which has since ended as well. Flanery played a young version of the Seelie Queen, a fairy with great power and control.

6 Isaiah Washington Has A Cooking Show

Isaiah Washington, who played Chancellor Jaha, has had a lot of controversy in the entertainment industry. He was fired from Grey’s Anatomy due to a homophobic insult directed at a fellow actor, but he has done steady work on television and in movies despite the scandal. In 2020, Washington joined the cast of the show P-Valley to play Mayor Tydell Ruffin.

He joined Fox News to create Isaiah Washington: Kitchen Talk. On the show, Washington chats with friends and celebrities while making a meal. Fans love how the show has put a twist on the interview-style.

5 Richard Harmon Joins Netflix’s Comedy Show Fakes

Richard Harmon’s John Murphy was a character fans loved to hate on The 100. He had a very interesting character arc, and ended up redeeming himself from his previous wicked ways. Harmon was a part of the CW show from its origin, and remained a main character through its end.

Now, Harmon is moving on from the drama genre and taking a chance at comedy. He has joined the cast of Netflix’s new comedy show Fakes, which will follow two teenage girls as they create a fake I.D. business. The show will hit the streaming network at the beginning of September.

4 Lindsey Morgan Leaves Walker

Lindsey Morgan’s Raven Reyes was a fan favorite on The 100. Fans loved her spunky attitude and how tough the character was, no matter how difficult the situation. Morgan appeared in every season of The 100 and was considered an integral part of the show.

Since The 100 came to a close, Morgan stayed with the CW network. She joined the cast of Walker, which is a re-boot of Chuck Norris’s portrayal of the Texas Ranger. Morgan played Micki Ramirez, though she has decided to leave the show. The show stars Jared Padalecki and a spinoff called Walker: Independence will air this fall.

3 Marie Avgeropoulos Loves Supporting Charity

Marie Avgeropoulos played the adored Octavia Blake on the CW’s The 100. Avgeropoulos has not done much acting since the show’s end, but she did star in the film Jiujitsu in 2020 and will be in the upcoming film King of Killers.

Outside of acting, Avgeropoulos is a big supporter of charity work. She works closely with Food For Life in Vrindavan, India, and raises funds for the charity. She feels strongly about empowering women around the globe. Her charity work often causes fans to forget she was once arrested for minor abuse.

2 Bob Morley’s Show Love Me

Bob Morley played Bellamy Blake. Viewers loved his character and were heartbroken when Bellamy was killed off by Clarke Griffin just three episodes before the series finale. Morley’s absence from the series finale was not the only thing he missed in season 7. Morley chose to take time off for personal reasons, resulting in his character’s absence for most of the final season.

Since his break from acting, Morley appeared in the 2021 mini-series Love Me. He is also a star of the upcoming film I’ll Be Watching, which will also star his wife Eliza Taylor.

1 Eliza Taylor Is A New Mom

Eliza Taylor and Bob Morley shocked the world in 2019 when they revealed they had not only been dating for years behind the scenes, but were also married! Fans of The 100 had wanted the two to get together, which likely caused the celebrity couple to keep their relationship a secret until they were officially husband and wife.

In 2021, the couple welcomed their first child. Taylor has spent most of her time since The 100 with her husband and new baby, but she will be in I’ll Be Watching with her husband.
