Early Life and College Career

Myles Garrett, born and raised in Arlington, Texas, had a passion for football from a young age. He attended Martin High School, where he quickly made a name for himself as a standout athlete. Along with excelling in football, Garrett also demonstrated his skills in other sports.

After graduating from high school, Garrett took his talents to Texas A&M University, where he played college football. His time at Texas A&M proved to be a defining period in his career. Garnering attention for his exceptional athleticism and impressive performances on the field, Garrett earned numerous accolades.

One of the notable honors he received was the Bill Willis Award in 2015, which recognizes the nation’s top defensive lineman. Additionally, Garrett was named first-team All-SEC in both 2015 and 2016, solidifying his status as one of the finest players in college football.

The culmination of his college career came in 2017 when Garrett decided to enter the NFL Draft. His exceptional skills and accomplishments caught the attention of NFL scouts, and he was ultimately selected as the first overall pick by the Cleveland Browns. This marked the beginning of his professional football journey.
