Kanye West Goes Off On Nas, Pusha T, And More In Leaked 2018 Recording

Footage from an apparent Kanye West documentary that was recorded around 2018 surfaced online this week, showcasing the Chicago producer going off on Nas, Pusha T, Teyana Taylor, and more.

The video shows Ye on the phone with someone he calls “Scooter,” presumably his former manager Scooter Braun. The 46-year-old appeared incensed and seemingly unhappy with his situation at G.O.O.D Music at the time.

“It ain’t gon’ be in this situation,” he said passionately. “It’s going to be a get me out this motherf**kin’ G.O.O.D Music sh*t now. And Scooter, it ain’t going to be no ‘I’m still putting my name on that sh*t’. I need to get rid of G.O.O.D Music ‘cause I’m great and guess what? Good is the enemy of great.”

Yeezy then turned his attention to the infamous Wyoming recording sessions and the artists that he worked with. “What the f**k I’m doing giving ‘Wanna Love You’ to f**king Teyana?” he asked in reference to her popular 2018 record “Gonna Love Me.” West asked the same question about King Push and his Grammy-nominated 2018 LP Daytona.

West was livid that he gave away what he believes to be the equivalent of three comparable albums to his 2010 classic, My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy. He then turned his attention to Nas, saying “’Cop Shot The Kid,’ Nas rapping all god damn off-beat on it. Don’t even wanna shoot a video then shoot the video and don’t even tell me. These motherf**kers don’t appreciate me. All these motherf**kers tryna use me. I’m the greatest motherf**king artist living and I can do everything!” Check out the video above.

In another video, Ye claims Cardi B is an Illuminati plant and calls her out for not writing her lyrics. “She don’t write her raps. She’s just there to sound as ignorant as possible and then make songs like, ‘F**k them and get some money,” he said. “She has literally replaced Nicki Minaj, purposely they put her there, and now she doesn’t know what to do, and she has no idea what the f**k is going on. She thinks it’s just a blessing from the universe. It ain’t no blessing from the universe.”

The Bronx superstar responded in a now-deleted tweet, adding a quote tweet with a heart to a clip of the Donda artist praising her during an interview with Jason Lee. “I’ve always believed in her since she was on [Love & Hip Hop],” he said on Hollywood Unlocked.
