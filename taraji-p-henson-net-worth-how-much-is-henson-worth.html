Early Life

Taraji P. Henson was born on September 11, 1970, in Washington, D.C. She grew up in a close-knit family with two siblings and shared a special bond with her grandmother. Henson’s upbringing played a significant role in shaping her character and values, instilling in her a strong work ethic and determination to succeed.

During her formative years, Henson attended high school where she discovered her passion for the performing arts. However, she initially pursued a different path, enrolling in North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University to study electrical engineering. Despite her academic pursuits, Henson’s love for acting remained steadfast, leading her to transfer to Howard University to pursue a career in the arts.

At Howard University, Henson honed her craft and immersed herself in the world of theater. She dedicated herself to her studies and participated in various productions, gaining valuable experience and recognition for her talent. It was during this time that Henson’s passion for acting grew, propelling her towards a successful career in the entertainment industry.

In her extensive filmography, Taraji P. Henson has showcased her ability to portray diverse characters with depth and authenticity. Some of her notable film roles include:

  • What Men Want (2019): Henson showcased her comedic skills in this comedy film, playing the role of a sports agent who gains the ability to hear men’s thoughts.
