Ryan O'Neal's Violent Altercation With His Son Griffin Was Just The Tip Of The Iceberg In Their Comp

Highlights

  • Ryan O'Neal's relationship with his children was filled with pain, addiction, and violence, leaving lasting scars on their lives.
  • Griffin O'Neal's toxic relationship with his father included multiple allegedly violent altercations.
  • Despite rumors of a reconciliation, Griffin remained estranged from Ryan until his 2023 death.

Ryan O'Neal died at the age of 82 on December 8th, 2023. After an extremely complicated life yet fairly successful acting and boxing career, O'Neal's time on this earth was cut short by a lengthy battle with cancer. News of his passing was broken on Instagram by his son, Patrick O'Neal. In the post, Patrick praised his father, calling him his hero. This sentiment was not shared by Patrick's siblings and half-siblings, least of all Griffin.

Griffin O'Neal, like his sister Tatum, was the product of Ryan's marriage to actor Joanna Moore. While Tatum's relationship with her scandal-embattled father was nowhere near perfect, Griffin's was positively toxic. This included at least two extremely violent altercations that represented the tip of the iceberg in terms of just how bad things were between the two.

Ryan O'Neal And His Son Griffin's Terrible Relationship Involved Violent Altercations

Pain, addiction, and heartache have plagued all four of Ryan O'Neal's children. This includes his youngest, Redmond O'Neal who suffers from addiction issues. The same is true for his older half-brother Griffin. According to People, Griffin had been surrounded by drugs and alcohol from a young age. He even claimed he was the family's "joint roller". Not only was his father an addict, but his mother, Joanna Moore, was also dependent on substances. This followed an extremely turbulent childhood of her own and a complicated relationship with both her then-husband and her career in Hollywood.

"I had to self-medicate my entire life because there was pain everywhere. There were drugs everywhere in my family all day, every day. It was the ’60s and ’70s and Tatum and I had a tough time."

According to People, Griffin became ensnared by substance addiction at the extremely young age of 9. By the time he graduated from high school, Griffin had already done two stints in rehab. Griffin's access to drugs and alcohol at a young age is one of the reasons why many believe Ryan was a bad father. In multiple interviews, Ryan himself claimed he wasn't "great" at being a dad aside from the fact that he was "hands-on".

There are conflicting reports as to when the first of at least two violent incidents occurred between Ryan O'Neal and his son, Griffin. People claimed it was in 1983 when Griffin was 16 years old. While other sources state that it was 1982, and Griffin was 18. But given that Griffin was born in 1964, he would have been closer to the age of 18 when his father allegedly punched his teeth out.

According to UPI, Griffin decided not to pursue formal assault charges after his father allegedly knocked out two of his teeth during a fight. Griffin claimed that he and his father got into an argument about playing racquetball, of all things. While little else has been revealed about this alleged event, a lot more was revealed about their extremely violent fight in 2007. This event fractured their relationship forever.

Was Ryan O'Neal Arrested For Assaulting His Son, Griffin?

Ryan O'Neal was arrested in 2007 after he allegedly shot a gun at his son, Griffin. According to TMZ, Ryan claimed that his son instigated the fight, accusing him of "wildly swinging a fireplace poker" in the living room of his Malibu home. Ryan's reps said that this resulted in Griffin's pregnant girlfriend sustaining injuries to her face.

In an interview with The Los Angeles Times, Ryan O'Neal stated that the poker grazed him multiple times before he retreated from the room to grab his firearm.
